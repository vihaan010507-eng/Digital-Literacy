# Case Study: Phishing Attack

## Overview
Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as 
passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted entity.

## Scenario
A college student receives an email that appears to be from their bank. The email says there is an urgent issue 
with their account and asks them to click on a link to verify their details. The message looks genuine, with the
bank’s logo and professional language.

## How It Happens
1. The attacker sends a fake email or message that looks official.  
2. The victim clicks on the provided link.  
3. The link opens a fake website that looks like the real one.  
4. The victim enters their login details or banking information.  
5. The attacker collects this data and uses it for unauthorized access.  

## Target Audience
Phishing attacks commonly target students, elderly people, and individuals who may not be fully aware of online 
security practices. Anyone using email or online banking services can become a target.

## Consequences
The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, unauthorized transactions, and identity theft.
Victims may also lose access to important accounts. In some cases, recovering lost money or data can be very difficult.

## Conclusion
This case highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking on links and sharing personal information online. 
Verifying the source and avoiding suspicious messages can help prevent such attacks.
